V1 TMR designed and fabricated their first single-seater, All-Terrain Vehicle(ATV) in the year 2009. The specifications of the car were as per the rules laid by SAE Baja. Single seated space frame chassis. Material used – AISI 4130 (chromoly). Welding used – tungsten inert gas arc welding (TIG). Special features : Roll stiffness, Ergonomics, Rider comfort and safety. Front suspension – SLA (short long arm) with nitrogen filled coil over oil shocks. Rear suspension – double wishbone suspension. 4×2 drive with half-shaft transmission, CVT to attain infinite gear ratios. Engine – BRIGGS AND STRATTON engine, 10hp OHV Intek model. Brakes – Single caliper, twin piston disk brakes, outward brakes on front wheels, and inboard braking on rear. Front-rear braking system, tandem master cylinder. Steering – Rack and pinion type steering system with turning radius of 10 feet. Special features of our car : 1. Off-terrain vehicle. 2. Chassis designed for optimum performance and driver comfort with safety concerns. 3. Suspension designed keeping in mind the terrain to provide best possible ride quality. 4. High performance Disc brakes employed to ensure efficient braking even under severe conditions like mud bogs, rock crawl, etc. 5. Most importantly, it’s a vehicle manufactured by students having first hand experience in manufacturing.

V3 was made in the year 2012 to participate in SAE BAJA held in Winconsin. Suspension-4130 chromoly steel Welding-TIG (Tungsten Inert  Gas)  arc  welding Steering-rack and pinion assembly, turning radius of 9ft Master Cylinder-Tandem master cylinder Brake calipers-dual hydraulic; (twin piston)   Special features Hydraulic disc brakes were preferred over drum brakes One of India’s lightest BAJA car through extensive use of advanced, lightweight materials. Inclusion of an onboard speedometer Limited Slip Differential (LSD) has drastically improved turning performance and stability

V4 Specifications SLA front suspension. Three-link rear suspension with passive toe-control. Fox Float™ air springs and dampers on all four corners. CVT-Consists of a Polaris P-90 42” belt Offers a ratio of 12:1, with a 2.8” travel per 360° of pinion rotation Aluminium steering arms, thinner carbon fibre tie rods, lighter EN24 mounts, carbon fibre steering wheel   Improvements Better layout, reducing the mounting space, creating a smaller rear end in the chassis. Lower centre of gravity by mounting all components on the lowermost plane of the vehicle. Better selection of CVT springs and flyweights to ensure better tuning. Lighter assembly (by approximately 10%). Higher top speed due to optimization of gear ratio by better selection of parts.
